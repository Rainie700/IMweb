Undergraduate Affairs

Welcome to the Department of Information Management! We are committed to providing comprehensive student services to help undergraduate students succeed in academics, life, and career planning.

Academic Guidance

  • Individual Guidance: Each undergraduate student is assigned an academic advisor who assists with course selection, academic questions, learning path planning, and career development.
  • Course Selection and Planning: Academic advisors provide professional course recommendations based on students' interests and career goals, helping them maximize learning outcomes.
  • Career Planning: Advisors guide students in exploring future career directions and help them design academic and internship paths that align with long-term goals.

Life Support

  • Housing Options: A variety of housing options are available, including dormitories and rental information, to help students find suitable living environments.
  • Psychological Health and Counseling: The university has a professional counseling center that provides psychological health support, helping students cope with academic pressure, emotional issues, and more.
  • Campus Activities: The student association regularly organizes various on-campus activities, club events, and gatherings, helping students build social networks and improve their physical and mental well-being.
  • Life Skills Training: The university holds occasional workshops and seminars on time management, stress management, and other topics to help students balance life and learning.

Internship and Employment Counseling

  • Internship Opportunities: The department collaborates with various companies to offer internship opportunities related to information management, helping students gain workplace experience.
  • Company Information Sessions: Multiple career fairs are held each semester, inviting industry representatives to share trends and job market demands.
  • Career Workshops: Courses on resume writing, interview skills, and workplace communication are offered to help students prepare for workplace challenges.
  • Mock Interviews and Job Search Guidance: The department organizes mock interview events and provides one-on-one job search counseling to enhance students' job market competitiveness.
  • Alumni Network: Through the alumni network, students can gain more industry connections and advice, expanding their professional networks.
